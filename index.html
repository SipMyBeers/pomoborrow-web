HOW IT ACTUALLY WORKS

Five invisible sensors.
One complete picture of your day.

Every productivity app asks you to tell it what you're doing. PomoBorrow already knows — by fusing five independent data streams that your phone is already collecting. No extra hardware. No wearable. No input.

📍

Location Intelligence

STRONGEST SIGNAL

Your phone already knows when you arrive somewhere and when you leave. PomoBorrow listens for these "visit" events — not by polling GPS (which kills battery), but by using Apple's built-in visit detection that runs on the motion chip at near-zero power.

WHAT IT SEES You arrived at 33.749, -84.388 at 5:32 PM. You've been here 47 times. You labeled it "Gold's Gym" on day 3. → Activity: Workout
Learns your places automatically Creates invisible geofences around your top 19 locations Gets smarter every visit — confidence goes from 50% to 95%+

Motion Detection

SAFETY-CRITICAL

The M-series motion coprocessor in your iPhone classifies your physical state every second — stationary, walking, running, cycling, or driving. This runs on dedicated hardware with zero battery impact. When PomoBorrow detects driving, it's a hard override: you're commuting. No questions asked.

WHAT IT SEES Motion: automotive for 12 minutes → merged with location shift → Activity: Commute. Last 8 drives to gym averaged 14 min. Today's traffic: +6 min.
30-second smoothing window prevents false triggers Driving detection is instant — overrides all other signals Learns your commute patterns and travel times

Network Fingerprinting

PATENT PENDING

This is what makes PomoBorrow different from everything else. Your home WiFi has an Apple TV, two HomePods, and an iPad. Your office has a printer and a MacBook. The gym has Bluetooth speakers. These device signatures are unique to each place — even when GPS can't tell two rooms apart.

WHAT IT SEES mDNS scan: Dylan's MacBook Pro (companion-link), HP LaserJet (ipp), Conference Room TV (airplay) → matches "Office" fingerprint → Activity: Work
Scans 5 Bonjour service types simultaneously Distinguishes "home office" from "living room" — GPS can't 120-second grace period prevents false switches from WiFi blips

Ambient Sound

NEURAL ENGINE

Apple's on-device Neural Engine can classify 300+ sound types — keyboard typing, gym equipment, traffic, restaurant chatter, silence. PomoBorrow listens for ambient sounds (never records or stores audio) to confirm what you're doing. It also uses ShazamKit to detect what music you play during each activity.

WHAT IT SEES Sound classification: metallic_clanking (0.82) + music (0.91) → maps to Exercise. Shazam: Travis Scott — FE!N → tagged to Workout playlist.
Classifies every 3 seconds — not continuous recording Runs on the Neural Engine, not the CPU — near zero battery Builds your music taste profile per activity over time

THE FUSION

All five combine into one decision.

Every few seconds, PomoBorrow weighs all five signals — location (35%), motion (25%), devices (15%), sound (15%), patterns (10%) — applies cross-signal agreement bonuses, and produces a single confidence-scored activity classification. When confidence hits 85%, it switches automatically. Below that, it suggests. Below 50%, it just watches and learns.
